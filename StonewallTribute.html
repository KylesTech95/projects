What happened at Stonewall?

Here's a time line of The Stonewall Riots as well as events that followed:


  • 1953 - Former President Dwight D. Eisenhower deployed an exective order "banning homosexuals from working for the federal government, citing security risk. Thousands lost their jobs because of their actual or perceived sexual orientation."
  • 1949 - "Fater of the Gay Rights Movement",Harvard graduate, Frank Kameny obtained his master's degree in astronomy.
  • 1956 -"Fater of the Gay Rights Movement",Harvard graduate, Frank Kameny obtained his doctorate degree in astronomy.
  • 1957 - Kameny was fired from the Amry Map Service and tried to sue, but was unsuccessful. Since then he dedicated his life to fighting for gay rights.
  • 1970 - "Christopher Street Liberation Day" - New York's first Pride Parade which was held by a young activist. From here on out, each pride was held each June where the march started on Christopher Street and concluded in Central Park.
  • 1987 - "First showing of the AIDS Memorial Quilt". During the Stonewall movement, a large blanket with names of those who passed away from AIDS was quilted and covered a large area of the National Mall.
  • Late 1980's & early 1990s"same-sex couples saw the first signs of hope on the marriage front", according to History.com's Gay Marriage
  • 2015 - The Obergefell v. Hodges case: "the U.S. Supreme Court ruled that all state bans on same-sex marriage were unconstitutional, making gay marriage legal throughout America."
